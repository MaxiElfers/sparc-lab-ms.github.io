A new study lead by our project partner Tianyi Xiao from ETH Zürich introduces "VResin" - a tool that lets users create 3D sketch maps in virtual reality to better express spatial memory. By combining 2D surface sketching with mid-air drawing, VResin helps users make more accurate 3D sketches. SPARC and SIL Labs contributed methods for analyzing these sketch maps. Tested with 48 participants, the tool shows promising potential for use in areas like spatial cognition research and design. The findings are published in the _International Journal of Human-Computer Studies_.

[VResin: Externalizing spatial memory into 3D sketch maps](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S107158192400106X)
